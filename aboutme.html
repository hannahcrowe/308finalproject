About Me

Hello, I'm Hannah Crowe, a passionate and detail-oriented Media Arts & Design major at James Madison University, originally from Richmond, Virginia. I am currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science with a concentration in Interactive Design expecting to graduating in May of 2024.

The skill set I have acquired throughout my time within my major and concentration have prepared me to take on projects involving HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, Figma, and many other tools and technologies. I find immense joy and purpose in bringing creative visions to life through design. I love creating work that will be a memorable user experience.

Design and artistic expression has been a lifelong passion for me, rooted in my early years of growing up with a love for dance. From childhood throughout my time in college, dancing provided a unique avenue for creative exploration. Beyond structure forms, I've always found joy in crafting my own projects for pure enjoyment. Whether it's creating collages that blend different mediums or assembling sprapbooks, I enjoy bringing my imaginative ideas to life.

My portfolio is a testament to this passion and showcases a diverse display of projects I am proud to show throughout my time as a Media Arts and Design major. Each project actively shows my understanding of design's impact and the skills I have learned to create a user-centric design experience.

In addition to my academic endeavors, my passion project that I took on was my position as my sororities Arts and Crafts Chairman. This position allowed me to express my love for design creation where I used Adobe Photoshop and InDesign to craft unique designs to project onto and recreate on our banners. This experience allowed me to translate my digital design skills into tangible, larger-than-life expressions. This experience shows my commitment to pushing the boundaries of design and transforming digital concepts into immersive and unique visual experiences.
